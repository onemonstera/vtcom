Sony Canvas

A TV-mounted device that projects ambient, intelligent content onto the wall surrounding your television — transforming home sports viewing from passive consumption into a shared, social experience.

// the insight

Through interviews with casual sports fans, we discovered that the social experience is the viewing experience. The number one thing fans miss when watching at home isn't the screen size or the picture quality — it's the energy, the togetherness, and the feeling of being part of something bigger.

Today's living room fails them: everyone pulls out their phones, casual fans disengage because they don't understand what's happening, and the room becomes a collection of parallel solo experiences instead of a shared moment.

// the solution

Sony Canvas is a single device — inspired by the Xbox Kinect form factor — that mounts on top of any TV. Using a rear-facing short-throw projector, it creates three supplementary "canvases" on the wall surrounding the television: one to the left, one to the right, and one above.

These canvases display contextual, ambient content — live scores, trivia, rule explanations, player stats, social feeds, and celebration animations — without ever touching the broadcast itself.

// presets
  • Casual — trivia, fun facts, rule explanations. Built for fans who are there for the vibe, not the analytics.
  • Avid — parallel game scores, live brackets. For the fan with three other games they're tracking.
  • Fantasy — player stats, performance metrics. Your fantasy lineup, on the wall.
  • Dynamic — AI-selected content based on game context and room energy. Adapts in real time to what's happening.

Each canvas slot is individually customizable, following an Apple Watch complication model that is familiar and intuitive.

// ambient intelligence

What makes Sony Canvas different from a second-screen app is its intelligence layer — Ambient Intelligence (AmI). The device reads the game (surfacing content during stoppages and dimming during live play), reads the room (detecting energy levels through sound and proximity sensors), and adapts to who's watching. Solo viewer gets deep stats; a group of eight gets social trivia and bigger celebrations. It knows which team the room supports and celebrates accordingly.

A 3D depth sensor scans the wall behind the TV and automatically maps the optimal canvas layout, avoiding obstacles like shelves, lamps, and artwork. The device is portable: take it to a friend's house and it rescans and adapts in seconds.

The broadcast stays untouched. Sony Canvas is the room around the TV — everything that makes watching at home feel like watching together.
// privacy as a foundation

A front-facing camera captures the room's reactions during big moments — with privacy at the center. A physical mechanical shutter (not a software toggle) is visibly closed when not in use. A countdown timer warns the room before any capture. Photos are stored locally and never uploaded until the host explicitly chooses to share.

All room sensing and ambient intelligence runs on-device — no video ever leaves the home. Privacy is built into the hardware, not bolted on as an afterthought.

// why sony

No other company has Sony's combination of PlayStation (already in millions of living rooms), camera and sensor technology, short-throw projection expertise, TV manufacturing, and sports technology partnerships (Hawk-Eye, goal-line tech, optical tracking) under one roof. Sony Canvas is a natural convergence of existing strengths into a new product category.

// design principles
  • Enable socialization — never prevent users from talking to each other
  • Intentional presence — non-intrusive by default, intelligent when it appears
  • Enhance community — personalized and localized to the viewers in the room
  • Same experience for all — one shared view, no fragmented screens
  • Preserve the broadcast — the TV stays untouched; canvases are supplementary
